My story

Beyond the résumé

Somewhere along the way I got a little obsessed with the gap between what people ask for and what they actually need. Someone wants a bigger kitchen island. What they actually want is to feel like the host they picture themselves being. I never really got over noticing that, and now it's basically how I think about product too. Not "what did the user say," but what they actually meant, even if they couldn't quite say it. That's the part of the job I care about most.

Outside work you'll usually find me climbing, reading, or sketching out whatever idea's keeping me up, sometimes just drawing because it's nice to make something with my hands again.
